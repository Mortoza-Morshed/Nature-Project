Urban green space mapping is the process of identifying and assessing green areas in cities. It helps planners understand where these spaces are, their quality, and how accessible they are. Using technologies like GIS, remote sensing and taking local surveys, this mapping supports sustainable development, urban resilience, and creates healthier cities.

Green space mapping contributes to improving environmental quality in cities. Green areas help reduce air pollution, mitigate urban heat island effects, and improve water infiltration and retention, leading to cleaner air, cooler temperatures, and better stormwater management.

Access to green spaces has been linked to numerous health benefits, including reduced stress, improved mental health, increased physical activity, and enhanced social interactions. Mapping green spaces helps identify areas where residents can access nature, engage in recreational activities, and enjoy the therapeutic effects of green environments.

Urban green spaces act as refuges for native plants, animals, and insects, supporting biodiversity and ecological resilience. They provide habitats, food sources, and breeding grounds for diverse species, contributing to the overall health of urban ecosystems.

Green spaces play a crucial role in climate adaptation by reducing the heat island effect in cities. Trees provide shade, lower ambient temperatures, and cool the surrounding environment through evapotranspiration. They also capture and store carbon dioxide, mitigating greenhouse gas emissions and contributing to climate change mitigation efforts.

Well-designed green spaces enhance the visual appeal of cities, creating attractive landscapes, green corridors, and scenic views. They contribute to urban aesthetics, improve neighborhood character, and create a sense of place that residents and visitors appreciate.
